# Stop-Slop Rules for Creative Output

**Purpose:** Remove AI-generated prose patterns from ad copy, headlines, descriptions, and marketing content.

## Banned Phrases (Remove Immediately)

- "Let's explore..."
- "It's important to note..."
- "In today's world..."
- "At the end of the day..."
- "It goes without saying..."
- "Needless to say..."
- "As a matter of fact..."
- "In fact..." (unless truly necessary)
- "Arguably..."
- "Interestingly enough..."
- "For all intents and purposes..."
- "Take it to the next level..."
- "Transformative," "revolutionary," "groundbreaking" (clichés)
- "Tap into," "unlock," "unleash," "harness," "leverage"
- "Journey," "paradigm," "synergy," "ecosystem"
- "In this digital age..." / "in this day and age..."
- "On the other hand..." / "that being said..."

## Structural Anti-Patterns

❌ **Binary contrasts** — Avoid "X vs Y" framings or "not X, but Y"
- Bad: "Not just another SaaS tool, but your business partner"
- Good: "Purpose-built for remote teams that ship fast"

❌ **Negative listings** — Lists starting with what you DON'T do
- Bad: "We don't believe in cookie-cutter solutions..."
- Good: "Every plan customizes to your workflow"

❌ **Dramatic fragmentation** — Short. Choppy. Sentences. For. Effect.
- Bad: "Speed. Reliability. Trust."
- Good: "Fast, reliable, trustworthy"

❌ **Rhetorical setups** — "Ever wondered why...?" or "Have you ever..."
- Bad: "Ever struggled with outdated ad tools?"
- Good: "Modern teams need smarter ad tools"

❌ **False agency** — Attributing feelings/actions to abstract concepts
- Bad: "The tool understands your needs..."
- Good: "Adjust settings to match your workflow"

❌ **Narrator-from-a-distance voice** — Third-person commentary
- Bad: "One might notice that..."
- Good: "You'll notice instantly"

❌ **Passive voice** — Weak, responsibility-evasive
- Bad: "Budgets are optimized by the system"
- Good: "We optimize your budget daily"

## Sentence-Level Rules

✅ **Directness:** Make claims, not suggestions
- ❌ "You might want to consider..."
- ✅ "Increase your CTR by X%"

✅ **Rhythm:** Vary sentence length; avoid metronomic patterns
- ❌ "This is fast. This is reliable. This is secure."
- ✅ "Works in seconds. Never crashes. Enterprise-grade security."

✅ **Trust:** Respect reader intelligence; don't over-explain
- ❌ "Our advanced AI-powered machine learning algorithm..."
- ✅ "AI-driven optimization"

✅ **Authenticity:** Sound human, not corporate
- ❌ "Synergistic cross-platform integrations"
- ✅ "Works across all your tools"

✅ **Density:** Cut anything cuttable
- ❌ "Fundamentally transform the very core of..."
- ✅ "Transform..."

## Forbidden Punctuation & Techniques

- ❌ Em dashes (—) overused for emphasis
- ❌ Exclamation marks beyond 1 per copy block
- ❌ ALL CAPS words (unless brand voice requires it)
- ❌ "Wh-" sentence starters ("What if," "Why would," "Where have")
- ❌ Staccato fragmentation (see "Dramatic fragmentation" above)
- ❌ Lazy extremes ("always," "never," "everyone," "nobody")

## Quality Rating (Rate 1-10 on Each)

| Dimension | Question | Target |
|-----------|----------|--------|
| **Directness** | Does it make statements or ask questions? | 8+ |
| **Rhythm** | Is sentence length varied, not metronomic? | 8+ |
| **Trust** | Does it respect reader intelligence? | 9+ |
| **Authenticity** | Does it sound human? | 9+ |
| **Density** | Is everything necessary? | 8+ |

**Minimum pass:** 8/10 (80% average across dimensions). Below that = revise.

## Application to Ad Copy

**Headlines (max 30 chars):**
- ✅ Direct, active verb, one benefit
- ❌ Rhetorical questions, vague claims

**Descriptions (max 90 chars):**
- ✅ Clear action, specific result, human tone
- ❌ Clichés, passive voice, meta-commentary

**Callouts (max 25 chars):**
- ✅ Action or fact
- ❌ Emphasis crutches ("truly," "really," "very")
